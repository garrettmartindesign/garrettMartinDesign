USDA Redesign

Case Study: Government Homepage UI Redesign

Project Brief

The assignment was to redesign the user-interface of an official government website.

Context

Case study done for the University of Denver. We were assigned a team to collaborate on redesign the user-interface of a government website. The team decided on reconstructing the U.S. Department of Agriculture. The plan is to:

  • Develop and conduct a user research plan,
  • Information Architecture
  • Design a new global navigation
  • Design and adapt and new design system
  • User Testing

The Team

Garrett Martin | User Experience Designer & Interface Designer
Amanda Sahaida | User Experience Designer
Doqoye Jackson | User Experience Designer

My Role

Of the members, I had more experience in the design process and knowledge of various tools. Initiated activities and design insights we were able to produce valuable data from research. I introduced a new design system including a new logo, fresh colors, buttons, and overall ui overhaul -- expressing a better suited tone for the USDA.

Audience

Majority of the user for the USDA originate from the office/corporate scenes. Its is a great resources for civilians as well; however, the need to visit the USDA is targeted to forms submissions, departmental directory, news and updates, and information overload.

Information Archtitecure

The current home page and subsidiaries were found to be too cluttering and do not provide enough contextual navigation. The user often got lost in the site, over/underwhelmed, and exited the page. The image to the right is an inventory of the site pages. Research suggest we focus on reconstructing the global navigation that includes information from both the homepage and the Natural Resource Page. Highlighted in orange is the currently navigation user flow and inventory.

Problem

When users visit USDA.gov, we uncover that they felt disinterest and eventually frustrated when using the navigation and general browsing.

Hypothesis

We proposed that updating the sites global navigation to a more efficient system will less frustrate the user. Secondly, if adapting a new design system could help keep users' interested for longer, less boring, and modern way?*
